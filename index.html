What is RoadMap?

RoadMap is a free, community-built tool that lets you explore road collision data across Hampshire, Southampton and Portsmouth on an interactive map. You can filter by year and severity, compare year-on-year trends, and drill into individual incidents.

Where does the data come from?

All data comes from DfT STATS19 — the UK government's official record of injury road collisions reported to police. It is published as open data by the Department for Transport under the Open Government Licence.

This is historical data, not live. Each year's dataset is typically published 12–18 months after the year ends once police records are verified and processed. The data shown reflects collisions where at least one person was injured — minor damage-only incidents are not recorded.

Who built this?

RoadMap is part of the sadken.co.uk community data project — a set of open tools built to help residents, parish councils and local groups make better evidence-based decisions using public data that already exists but is hard to access.

It was built as a volunteer effort for the Lyndhurst & New Forest community, with no commercial interest. The code is open and the data is yours.

Limitations

  • Only injury collisions attended by police are recorded — near-misses and damage-only incidents are excluded.
  • Location precision depends on police GPS accuracy at the time of recording.
  • Data is updated annually, not in real time.
  • This tool is in Beta — more data layers are planned.
